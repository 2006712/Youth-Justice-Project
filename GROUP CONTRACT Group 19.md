Section 1: Terms and Conditions of Group Work

As a group we agree to work together in a respectful, responsible and professional manner. All of the members are expected to make a fair contribution to the project, including planning, coding, documenting, and reviewing. If every person is equally contributing, all of the members will get the same mark. However, if a member is not doing his share of the work this will be discussed as a group and supported with evidence such as GitHub commits and Teams messages.

For discussion and meetings, we will mostly use Microsoft Teams, and for file management and progress tracking, we will use GitHub. All members are expected to respond to one another within 24 hours and notify the group early if they cannot be available.

Tasks will be split during weekly meetings with several of the GitHub Issues or shared notes. If someone falls behind we will be there to first support them and if necessary adjust tasks. If this problem still persists, we will bring it up as a group and, if needed, tell the lecturer.

We understand that everyone has different schedules and responsibilities, therefore we will try and plan ahead and be flexible where possible. All work should adhere to the rules of academic integrity of CDU. If AI tools are used, the work should be then checked, understood, and agreed upon by all members of the group before the work is submitted.
